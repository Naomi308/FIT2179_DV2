Melbourne: A Liveable, Diverse and Culturally Active City

This dashboard explores why Melbourne is often perceived as a highly liveable city. It combines social indicators, neighbourhood liveability, cafes and restaurants, venue types, cultural diversity, pedestrian movement, and landmarks across the City of Melbourne.

Liveability

What makes Melbourne highly liveable?

This section compares Melbourne’s strongest liveability indicators, suburb-level performance, wellbeing profile and safety conditions. Together, these charts show how liveability is shaped by wellbeing, safety, cultural acceptance and daily urban experience.

Melbourne’s strongest liveability indicators

This chart highlights the indicators where Melbourne performs well, helping explain why the city is often seen as a desirable and liveable place.

Suburb liveability profile

Selected suburbs are compared across quality of life, daytime safety, night-time safety and wellbeing score.

Wellbeing profile

This visual shows the relative strength of different wellbeing dimensions, giving a more detailed view of residents’ quality of life.

Safety comparison

Safety is a key part of liveability. This chart separates perceived safety from broader safety and crime-related context.

Food, venues and urban activity

Where are Melbourne’s cafes, restaurants and venues concentrated?

This section explores Melbourne’s hospitality and venue landscape. The map shows where cafes and restaurants are located, while the bar chart summarises the broader venue mix across the city.

Cafes and restaurants map

This interactive map shows 2024 cafe and restaurant locations by seating capacity, supported by locality boundaries and the City of Melbourne municipal outline.

Venue types in Melbourne

Venues are grouped into broader categories such as restaurants, cafes, food retail, nightlife, leisure, fitness and transport to show Melbourne’s urban activity mix.

Diversity

Melbourne’s social and cultural diversity

Melbourne’s diversity is shown through country of birth, languages spoken and religious affiliation. These charts present diversity across multiple dimensions rather than relying on one single measure.

Diversity profile

The donut charts compare country of birth, non-English languages and religious affiliation. Percentages are recalculated after excluding broad residual categories.

City pulse

How does Melbourne move through time?

Pedestrian activity provides a direct view of the city’s daily rhythm. This chart allows users to zoom into selected time periods and compare movement patterns around major cultural and sporting events.

Melbourne’s daily pulse

Drag across the smaller overview chart to zoom into a specific time period on the main chart.

Landmarks and places of interest

Melbourne’s civic, cultural and community infrastructure

This map visualises landmarks and places of interest, including schools, theatres, health services, sports facilities, places of worship, galleries and museums.

Landmarks and places of interest

Users can zoom and filter by landmark group to explore the city’s cultural and civic infrastructure at a more detailed level.

Data sources: City of Melbourne Open Data and cleaned datasets prepared for the FIT2179 Data Visualisation project.
