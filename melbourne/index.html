Melbourne liveability

Liveability is built from safety, wellbeing and neighbourhood experience

This section moves from global reputation to local evidence. It compares neighbourhood liveability, wellbeing scores and safety perceptions across selected areas of Melbourne.

Suburb liveability profile

Selected suburbs are compared across quality of life, daytime safety, night-time safety and wellbeing score.

Wellbeing profile

This chart shows how wellbeing varies across local areas, helping explain the softer quality-of-life side of Melbourne’s liveability.

Safety comparison

Safety is a core part of liveability. This chart compares perceived safety across different contexts.

Food and culture

Melbourne’s food and cultural infrastructure supports its identity as a vibrant city

Melbourne’s reputation is strongly tied to its hospitality scene and cultural destinations. This section maps cafes, restaurants, landmarks, museums and places of interest, then summarises the city’s overall venue mix.

Cafes and restaurants map

This map shows 2024 cafe and restaurant locations by seating capacity, with locality boundaries and the City of Melbourne municipal outline.

Landmarks and cultural places

This map displays landmarks and places of interest, including theatres, galleries, museums, schools, health services, places of worship and sports facilities.

Venue types in Melbourne

Venues are grouped into broader categories such as restaurants, cafes, food retail, nightlife, leisure, fitness and transport to show Melbourne’s urban activity mix.

Diversity

Melbourne’s diversity is visible through origin, language and belief

This section presents diversity across three dimensions: country of birth, languages spoken and religious affiliation. Together, they show that Melbourne’s social identity is shaped by multiple cultural layers.

Diversity profile

The donut charts compare country of birth, non-English languages and religious affiliation. Percentages are recalculated after excluding broad residual categories.

Liveliness

Melbourne’s daily pulse shows how people move through the city

Pedestrian activity provides a direct measure of the city’s rhythm. This chart allows users to zoom into specific periods and compare movement patterns around major sporting and cultural events.

Melbourne’s daily pulse

Drag across the smaller overview chart to zoom into a specific time period on the main chart.
