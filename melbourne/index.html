Melbourne liveability

Liveability is built from safety, wellbeing and neighbourhood experience

Liveability in Melbourne is not only about being highly ranked. It is felt at street level: through safety, wellbeing, neighbourhood character and the comfort of moving through different parts of the city.

Suburb liveability profile

Melbourne does not feel the same everywhere. Each suburb contributes a different version of the city, with some areas standing out for safety, others for wellbeing, and others for overall quality of life.

Wellbeing profile

Wellbeing adds a more human layer to Melbourne’s liveability story. It shows that a successful city is not only functional, but also capable of supporting how people feel in their everyday environment.

Safety comparison

A city’s liveability changes across the day. Comparing safety perceptions helps show where Melbourne feels comfortable, and where the experience of the city may become more uneven after dark.

Food and culture

Melbourne’s food and cultural infrastructure supports its identity as a vibrant city

Melbourne’s identity is closely tied to what people do outside home and work. Its cafes, restaurants, cultural venues and public destinations create the social infrastructure that makes the city feel active and distinctive.

Cafes and restaurants map

Food is one of Melbourne’s most visible urban signatures. The spread of cafes and restaurants shows how dining is embedded into the city’s streets, not limited to a few tourist precincts.

Landmarks and cultural places

Melbourne’s cultural life is not only represented by major landmarks. It is also built through theatres, galleries, museums, schools, places of worship, health services and everyday community spaces.

Venue types in Melbourne

The variety of venues shows how Melbourne functions as more than a business district. Restaurants, cafes, nightlife, leisure, fitness and transport spaces all contribute to the rhythm of the city.

Diversity

Melbourne’s diversity is visible through origin, language and belief

Melbourne’s diversity is one of the clearest reasons the city feels international without losing its local character. Country of birth, language and belief reveal how many different communities are woven into the city’s social fabric.

Diversity profile

Diversity in Melbourne is not a single statistic. It appears across where residents were born, the languages spoken at home and the beliefs people identify with, creating a layered picture of belonging.

Liveliness

Melbourne’s daily pulse shows how people move through the city

The movement of people through the city gives Melbourne its daily pulse. Pedestrian activity helps reveal when the city feels busy, where people gather, and how major events change the rhythm of urban life.

Melbourne’s daily pulse

Pedestrian patterns turn the city into a timeline. By zooming into different periods, we can see how Melbourne’s streets respond to ordinary weekdays, weekends, sporting events and cultural moments.
